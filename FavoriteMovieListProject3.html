List Of My Favorite Movies

  • 1. The Notebook (2004)

    Genre: Romance, Drama This heartfelt love story follows Noah and Allie, two young people from different social backgrounds who fall deeply in love during the summer of 1940. Despite separation, family pressure, and war, their love stands the test of time. The story is told through an old man reading a diary to a woman in a nursing home, slowly revealing a beautiful truth about enduring love and memory.

  • 2. The Pursuit of Happyness (2006)

    Genre: Biography, Drama Based on a true story, this movie follows Chris Gardner, a struggling salesman who becomes homeless with his young son. Despite the hardships, Chris never gives up. Through determination, love for his child, and strong willpower, he fights for a better future. It's a powerful story about chasing dreams, overcoming struggles, and the meaning of real success.

  • 3. The Shawshank Redemption (1994)

    Genre: Drama, Crime This classic film tells the story of Andy Dufresne, a banker who is wrongly convicted of murder and sent to Shawshank prison. There, he befriends another inmate, Red, and together they discover the meaning of hope, friendship, and inner freedom. It’s a deeply moving film that shows how the human spirit can survive even in the darkest places.

  • 4. Titanic (1997)

    Genre: Romance, Drama Set aboard the ill-fated RMS Titanic, this movie is a love story between Jack, a poor artist, and Rose, a wealthy young woman. Their romance grows during the ship’s maiden voyage, but it’s tragically interrupted by the ship's sinking. The film beautifully combines historical tragedy with powerful emotions, stunning visuals, and timeless music.

  • 5. 3 Idiots (2009)

    Genre: Comedy, Drama This Indian film explores the pressure-filled lives of engineering students in a highly competitive college. Through the journey of three close friends, especially the free-spirited Rancho, the movie challenges traditional ideas of success and education. It teaches us to follow passion over pressure, and to learn with joy rather than fear. Full of humor, emotion, and life lessons
